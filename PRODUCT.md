# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Students in Peru preparing for a university admission exam ("preuniversitarios"), typically finishing secondary school or enrolled in an intensive prep academy ("academia preuniversitaria"). They're targeting one or more specific universities (UNI, UNMSM/San Marcos, PUCP, UNALM, UNFV, etc.), each with its own exam format, date, and course weighting. Admi-Tec now covers every course on the admission exam, not just math — students study across the full subject list their target university tests. Their context is focused, repeated study under a visible deadline: short topic drills mixed with full timed simulacros, plus rendering real past official exams to know exactly what the real thing feels like.

## Product Purpose

Admi-Tec helps preuniversitario students prepare for their admission exam across every subject it covers, giving them real past official exams and simulacros to rehearse under actual exam conditions, and a public ranking so they can see exactly where they stand against other students targeting the same exam. Success looks like: a student arrives knowing the platform has genuine, complete coverage (not just one subject), trusts the exams are the real thing, and can tell — from the ranking — whether they're actually ready.

## Positioning

Admi-Tec is the one platform where a student rinde real past official admission exams and simulacros across every course their exam covers, and sees exactly how they compare to other postulantes on a live ranking — not a single-subject drill tool, and not a black box about where they stand.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary and secondary CTA: registrarse / crear cuenta gratis (primary); ver el ranking público (secondary, for visitors not yet ready to register).
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: exámenes oficiales reales + simulacros de todos tus cursos, y un ranking que te dice dónde estás parado.
- Belief ladder: (1) los exámenes son reales, no inventados — condiciones y formato del examen oficial; (2) es gratis y fácil empezar, sin fricción para probar; (3) puedo ver dónde estoy parado frente a otros postulantes gracias al ranking.

## Brand Personality

Bold and competitive rather than purely warm — "modo examen": the public site's job is to put a visitor into the headspace of the actual exam moment (real timers, a live ranking, the weight of the countdown) and make them want to know where they stand. This is a step beyond the prior "riguroso, cercano, claro" academia-teacher warmth: the tone now leans into urgency and competition, while staying credible and substantive rather than promotional or gimmicky. The authenticated practice app (post-signup) keeps a calmer, focused "study notebook" register once a student is inside working — the boldness is a public-site trait, not something that should leak into mid-drill or mid-exam screens where calm focus still matters most.

## Anti-references

No specific named anti-references given. Most of the boundary carries over unchanged even with the bolder tone: no cartoon mascots, no game-like badges or achievement pop-ups. Competitive/urgent ≠ gamified/toy-like — the ranking and countdowns should read as real instrumentation (exam timers, a leaderboard), not playful game mechanics. One deliberate exception (added when the landing page's motion was revisited): a brand-colored confetti burst and a springy button/card overshoot are allowed specifically on the primary "Crear cuenta gratis" conversion action and its surrounding interactive elements — celebrating the one moment that matters (signup), not decorating the page. This doesn't extend to the ranking or countdown, which stay competitive-not-gamified as before. See DESIGN.md's "CTA Bounce Exception."

## Design Principles

- **Exams are real, so they should look real.** Timers, scores, and rankings read as instrument data (tabular figures, exam-sheet precision), not decorative UI — this is what makes "los exámenes son reales" believable at a glance.
- **Full coverage must be legible, not implied.** With every admission course now in scope, any screen listing subjects, filters, or recommendations must make the breadth obvious — a visitor shouldn't have to dig to learn this isn't just a math tool.
- **Competitive, not gamified.** The ranking and countdown urgency should feel like real stakes (a genuine exam is coming), never like a game layer bolted onto studying.
- **The public site sells the moment; the app delivers the work.** The bold navy-and-amber "exam sheet" register belongs to the public/marketing surface. Once a student is inside practicing or mid-exam, the calmer notebook register takes over — don't merge the two.
- **One university, one context.** A student can target multiple universities with different exams and dates; every screen showing content, filters, or recommendations must make it unambiguous which university's context is being shown.
- **Anonymity is a feature, not an afterthought.** The ranking is opt-in and pseudonymous by design — never leaks real names or identifying data, even while it drives competitive motivation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Practical WCAG AA-level defaults: verified color contrast (worth double-checking given the darker navy/amber public-site palette), comfortable touch targets, and reduced-motion alternatives for any animation (the site already has marquee and glow effects to cover). No formal compliance mandate or additional specific accommodations requested beyond this baseline.
